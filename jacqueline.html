Time Series Models

On this page, we will try to predict the average weekly sales by the top 5 most and least profitable stores, respectively. Using grouping calculations, we found that the most profitable stores overall were stores 2, 4, 13, 14, and 20. The least profitable stores were 3, 5, 33, 38, and 44.

First, we created linear models showing the changes in average weekly sales per month.


For the most part, the top five most profitable stores have pretty similar sales trends, with numbers spiking during the holiday season. Business is fairly standard, if not completely stable, for these locations.

It's a different story for the least profitable stores, which jump all over the place at varying points and have different shapes per store. Perhaps because these locations struggle more financially, their leadership applies marketing tactics that greatly alters the weekly sales, regardless of the time of year. Store 38 had a marked improvement during the last several months, perhaps a result of new strategies that benefitted it.

Knowing how the most and least profitable stores fare overall, we will now build time series models to predict their values.

First, we're going to show the average weekly sales before differences transformations, both for the most and least profitable stores. Essentially, this is just combining the five lines from each respective model into one.

As was the case before, the most profitable stores have more consistent-looking trends than the least, which don't spike as much during the holiday season and have an overall increase during the second half of the time period.
